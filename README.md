# Safe Tech Cyber (STC) — Response Frameworks

Welcome to the official public repository for **Safe Tech Cyber (STC)** response architectures and Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs). This repository houses our technical blueprints designed for time-critical passive threat intelligence and digital recovery operations.

## 📌 What is This?
This repository serves as the central documentation hub for STC's operational workflows. It provides hyper-detailed, step-by-step procedural guidelines for our Threat Intelligence analysts and Legal Compliance teams during critical investigative windows.

## 🚨 Why Does This Exist?
In critical events like child abductions or human trafficking, the first 24 to 48 hours are statistically vital for successful recovery. Standard law enforcement frameworks are often overwhelmed or constrained by initial processing delays. 

STC bridges this gap by designing **Data Protection by Design** frameworks that allow private technical assets to securely, legally, and passively extract open-source intelligence (OSINT). This data is compiled into cryptographically sealed evidence packets ready for immediate law enforcement handoff.

## ⚙️ How It Operates
Our frameworks are built around three core pillars:
1. **Hardened Operational Security (OpSec):** Total technical isolation using sandboxed Kali Linux environments, multi-hop network masking, and thoroughly sanitized investigative personas to protect the integrity of the mission.
2. **Data-Engine Segmentation:** Eliminating wasted operational time by routing visual intelligence strictly through advanced facial-recognition infrastructure (Yandex Images/PimEyes) and text/infrastructure intelligence through highly targeted Google Dorking and network mapping tools (Shodan).
3. **Rigid Constitutional Alignment:** Absolute compliance with United Kingdom and European legal statutes. Our operations bypass standard data constraints using the **Vital Interests** exemptions under UK GDPR Articles 6 and 9, alongside the Crime and Detection exemptions within the **UK Data Protection Act 2018**.
